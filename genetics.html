What I Did

In this genetics project, I explored how traits are passed from parents to offspring. I studied patterns of inheritance and learned how genes affect characteristics like appearance and traits. I used models and examples to understand how genetic information is passed down.

I also completed activities where I predicted outcomes of genetic crosses using basic probability. I worked through Punnett squares and practiced identifying dominant and recessive traits. This helped me understand how genetics works in real life situations.

What I Learned

I learned that genetics explains why people and organisms have different traits. DNA carries instructions that control how living things develop and function. This helped me understand how biology is connected to inheritance.

I also learned how scientists use models like Punnett squares to predict traits. This showed me that biology uses logic and probability to understand real-world patterns in living organisms.

Skills I Improved

I improved my critical thinking skills by analyzing genetic problems and solving Punnett squares. I also improved my ability to follow scientific steps carefully.

I also improved my understanding of how to use data and patterns to make predictions. This helped me think more like a scientist and not just memorize information.
