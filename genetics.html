What I Did

In this project, I learned about how scientists analyze DNA using gel electrophoresis and PCR. I studied how PCR is used to copy small sections of DNA so that scientists can have enough material to analyze.

I also learned how gel electrophoresis separates DNA fragments based on size. I learned how DNA moves through a gel when an electric current is applied, with smaller pieces moving faster than larger ones.

What I Learned

I learned that PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction) is a process that makes millions of copies of DNA quickly. This is important in science and medicine because it allows researchers to study very small amounts of DNA.

I also learned that gel electrophoresis is used to sort DNA fragments by size and create a DNA pattern or “DNA fingerprint.” This is used in forensics, genetics, and medical testing.

Skills I Improved

I improved my understanding of scientific processes and how lab techniques work step-by-step. I also improved my ability to follow scientific diagrams and models.

I also improved my critical thinking skills by connecting how PCR and gel electrophoresis are used in real-world biology applications like crime scene analysis and genetic testing.
